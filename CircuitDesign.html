An improved version of the Bare Bones Arduino using a TQFP package of the ATMega. The hardest part was routing pins 26-32 (top right portion) of the ATMega. I ended up connecting these on the bottom side of the board. Compared to Version 1 of the design, this was meant as a breakout and a learning excercise for making schematics with SMT components. The board was 1.67x1.79 inches (2.99 sq. inch) and uses the 16 Mhz Resonator (which I forgot to label). Thinking back I should have removed the power jack. A future version will probably have a power LED, use 10 mil traces, and use an IDC cable to further reduce the size of the board. The only unfortunate oversight I had was not ensuring that the pins were correctly spaced to insert into a breadboard. I also want to see if using an SMT version of the oscillator with caps would make it easier to route compared to the 3-pin oscillator used.
